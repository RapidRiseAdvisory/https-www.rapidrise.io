FAQ for Technical Founders & VCs

Why do technical founders need a commercial operator?

Technical founders excel at building exceptional products. But selling them at enterprise scale requires different expertise.

Enterprise buyers—CISOs, CFOs, Risk Officers—do not evaluate technical products the way engineers do. They care about compliance, risk mitigation, procurement process, and ROI justification.

I remove revenue responsibility from founders while preserving their involvement in strategic deals. I handle enterprise procurement, MSAs, security questionnaires, and pilot-to-production execution so founders can focus on product and vision.

What does "revenue architecture" mean for a Series A/B venture?

Revenue architecture is the operational infrastructure that makes revenue repeatable and predictable.

For Series A/B technical ventures, this means:

  • Enterprise sales playbooks tailored to complex technical products
  • Pricing and packaging designed for pilot-to-production cycles
  • CRM configuration for long, multi-stakeholder sales processes
  • Pipeline forecasting frameworks that boards and VCs trust
  • Sales compensation and hiring standards that scale

This is not theory. This is installation and execution.

When should a technical founder transition away from founder-led sales?

When founder involvement becomes the bottleneck, not the advantage.

Signs you need to transition:

  • Pipeline stalls when the founder is unavailable
  • Sales cycles are extending because founders cannot scale time
  • Board is asking for predictable forecasting and the founder cannot provide it
  • First sales hire failed because there was no process to hand off
  • Series B conversations require demonstrated commercial maturity

I professionalize sales without losing speed or culture. Founders stay involved in strategic deals. I handle execution, process, and team building.

How do you navigate enterprise procurement for technical products?

Enterprise procurement for technical products is not about better demos. It is about risk mitigation, compliance approval, and multi-stakeholder alignment.

I handle:

  • CISO security reviews and SOC 2 / ISO 27001 discussions
  • CFO budget justification and ROI modeling
  • Legal negotiations for MSAs, DPAs, and liability caps
  • Risk Officer approvals for critical infrastructure or AI systems
  • Pilot design that leads to production contracts, not endless POCs

This is where technical products get stuck. I unstick them.

What metrics matter for Series B readiness?

Series B investors do not fund potential. They fund demonstrated execution and predictable growth.

The metrics that matter:

  • Repeatable enterprise deals closed without founder dependency
  • Pipeline coverage ratio (3x+ for technical sales cycles)
  • Forecast accuracy within 10-15% quarter over quarter
  • Sales efficiency metrics (CAC payback, win rates by segment)
  • Evidence of scalable process: playbooks, CRM discipline, hiring standards

I install the systems that produce these metrics. Not spreadsheets. Actual operational infrastructure.

How do you structure engagements with technical founders?

I do not consult. I execute.

Typical engagement structure:

  • I take operational ownership of revenue execution
  • I work directly with founders and report to the board
  • I install CRM, playbooks, forecasting, and hiring processes
  • I close the first institutional deals that prove scalability
  • Duration is typically 6-12 months, covering Series A to Series B transition

This is interim VP of Sales execution, not advisory. I am accountable for outcomes, not recommendations.
